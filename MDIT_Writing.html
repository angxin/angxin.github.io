Malaysia Data Innovation Talent X DOSM Datathon 2023

In September 2023, I participated in the Malaysia Data Innovation Talent (MDIT) Datathon. It is a national competition for university students hosted by Universiti Teknologi Mara (UiTM) and Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM). The goal of the datathon is to use open-source government data to create a dashboard and present a solution to a Sustainable Development Goal (SDG).

Our team, Lionhearts, consisted of me and my teammates, Chan Hui Wen, Ewen Lim Yuan-Khai and Ng Jun Fei under the guidance of our lecturer Dr Gloria Teng Ai Hui. During the course of the datathon, we created a PowerBI dashboard, a comprehensive report and presented a live pitch to a panel of judges. Out of 100 teams that participated, I am proud to say our team earned 3rd place!

Preliminary Round

The competition officially started on 28th August 2023. We had to submit our dashboard and report within a week, so we quickly got started searching for data and choosing our topic. Our group chose to focus on air pollution in Malaysia which covers SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being) and SDG 13 (Climate action).

Final Round

We were one of the top ten groups invited to pitch at Bank Negara Malaysia and were given one week from the announcement to prepare for the presentation.

We were editing and re-editing the script and slides throughout the week to ensure all the important information was included within the 10 minutes allocated. We also had rehearsals among ourselves and under the supervision of Dr Gloria who gave us constructive feedback on our presentation.

Finally the day had come! Despite feeling nervous, I pushed on and presented with my team in front of the judges, who were data scientists from various agencies like Bank Negara Malaysia and DOSM.

We gave a detailed explanation on the functionality of the dashboard as shown in the video below. We also discussed problems that poor air quality causes, along with the key findings of our dashboard and possible solutions to implement.

The whole event was live streamed on facebook which had Session 1 and Session 2. We got to watch amazing presentations from other groups and saw various approaches to their respective SDG problems.

In the end after all the weeks of hardwork, we successfully emerged with 3rd place!

Teamwork and presentation skills were also an important part in getting us towards our great results. I am grateful to my team and supervisor for putting so much effort into our project and performance. Overall, it was an amazing experience and I hope to use my experience to complete even more impactful projects.
